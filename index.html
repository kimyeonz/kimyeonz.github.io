About

I am a M.S. student at the Graduate School of Metaverse, KAIST. My research interests lie at the intersection of natural language processing and computational social science, with a focus on understanding and explaining online polarization and social media behavior.


Research Interests

  • Explainable AI (XAI) for Online Polarization Detection — Developing explainable models to identify coordinated manipulation and polarization-inducing behavior in social media discourse, leveraging moral emotion signals.
  • LLM Probing for Social Media Understanding — Investigating internal representations of large language models to understand how socially and morally sensitive online content is encoded.
  • AI Agent-based Modeling of Public Opinion Formation — Building multi-agent simulations with moral emotion attributes to study how public opinion and polarization emerge on social media.
